Adam Korga

Self-proclaimed writer

Survived 20 years in IT. Mostly intact.

A pen name. I try not to pull punches — though I do flinch when they come back.

I’ve been a developer, DevOps tinkerer, Scrum Master, and reluctant team lead. I worked in startups that moved fast and broke things — and in enterprises that moved slow and broke people.

After two decades of production fires, buzzwords, and PowerPoint‑driven decisions, I decided it was time to document the madness — with just enough sarcasm to survive.

No, I won't tell you which parts are based on real conversations. You already know.

IT Dictionary

A survival manual for devs, dreamers, and those still pretending their job has structure.

"This satirical workplace guidebook is a laugh-out-loud group therapy session that offers technical and emotional support by decoding corporate-speak."
— Independent Book Review

From debugging trenches and absurd Agile rituals, through Corpoland's soul‑crushing bureaucracy, to Startupistan's hoodie‑fueled chaos and AI's confident hallucinations — IT Dictionary is your darkly funny survival manual for life in modern tech.

language of IT into what people actually mean:

  • Part 1: Core IT – Survival Mode Activated:
    Production fires, polite blame games, and the art of surviving 3 AM incidents.
  • Part 2: Agile Rituals and Other Cargo Cults:
    Stand‑ups, retros, and the ceremonial theater of "velocity."
  • Part 3: Corpoland – Where Ideas Go to Get Approved:
    PowerPoint-driven decisions, buzzword currency, and bureaucratic purgatory.
  • Part 4: Startupistan – Chaos, Hoodie and Deadline:
    Fake metrics, heroic all‑nighters, and pivots faster than coffee cools.
  • Part 5: AI – The New Kid in Town:
    Confident hallucinations, vibe coding, and the shiny new messiah of tech.

This is no "special offers in your inbox" kind of deal. It's a weekly newsletter — one essay, one topic, in my usual style.

Expect things like:

  • Tech explained so even a 7-year-old would understand but an engineer wouldn't cry
  • Legendary engineering screw-ups and the lessons hidden inside
  • Workplace absurdities worth laughing at (while secretly documenting for survival)
  • A dose of systems thinking without the buzzword bingo or consultant speak

In other words: the same mix of satire and clarity you'll find in my books, but fresh every week.

What you won't get: spam, affiliate junk, or "exclusive offers" nobody asked for.

Just one honest essay each week. Fun, sharp, and (hopefully) useful.
